About

I'm a senior product manager who builds. Fifteen years in regulated financial platforms — transaction processing, settlement, equity compensation — taught me that the most useful tools are usually the quietest. They reduce noise rather than add features.

My work sits at the intersection of product, engineering, and operations. I design strategies, then write the code to prove them. I treat internal operations as "customer zero" — if a tool can't earn the trust of the people closest to the work, it has no business reaching the people farther away.

I care about craft, calm interfaces, durable systems, and the small details that signal someone was paying attention.

Principles

Four ideas I keep
returning to.

Systems thinker. Quiet innovator. Built on the 3 I’s — plus one.

01

Integrity

Compliance, safety, and data isolation belong in the build pipeline — not the review queue. Trust is earned by the small choices, made consistently, when no one is watching.

02

Intelligence

Think in systems, not in features. Reduce complexity by removing parts, not adding more. Read the whole pattern before writing the spec — the most elegant solution is usually the quietest one.

03

Initiative

Don’t manage builders — be one. Hands-on craftsmanship beats committee-driven design. The proof of the idea is the shipped product, not the slide deck.

04

Impact

Treat internal operations as customer zero. Validate value with the people closest to the work before scaling outward. Measurable outcomes over measured activity.
